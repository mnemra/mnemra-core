## JTBD

After `0.14.0`'s one-shot corpus migration, content keeps arriving — markdown notes, binary documents (PDF/PPTX/XLSX/DOCX/CSV/JSON), later clipped web content and feed items. Today nothing ingests it: **new content enters mnemra only if a human notices it and re-runs something by hand**, and every derived view of it is re-computed per-session — the exact re-derivation cost mnemra exists to kill.

The job: **content arriving in the corpus enters mnemra durably and automatically, so that derived context can be (re)computed from what was persisted rather than re-ingested** — the corpus stays current as content arrives, without an agent or human having to notice, and a new derivation (new embedding model, new extractor) re-runs over already-persisted content with zero re-fetch. (The raw-first-with-provenance mechanism that delivers this is hard constraint 2, kept out of the job statement.)

## Success criteria

1. **One loader seam, two drivers.** The parse/normalize/frontmatter-map/provenance-stamp/write-through machinery is a single named seam; the `0.14.0` one-shot walker and the `1.2.0` arrival-driven drivers both drive it. *Observable:* the Frame names the seam contract; the design **admits** both drivers (building the `0.14.0` driver itself is out of scope per Non-goal 1 — the seam must not preclude it) with no duplicated loader logic; the migrator-vs-ingest differences (kill-and-resume manifest, byte-equivalence, source-read-only vs. arrival detection, dedup, recency) live entirely in the drivers.
2. **Raw-first durability (ELT).** Arriving content persists raw with provenance BEFORE any derivation; raw is truth, derived is disposable projection. *Observable:* delete all derived output, re-run transforms → derived repopulates from persisted raw alone, with no read of the arrival source. (Output byte-identity is deliberately NOT the property — transforms may be non-deterministic; whether any transform pins determinism is a Frame question.)
3. **Re-runnable derivation, no re-ingest.** A new derive version (new extractor, new embedding model) re-runs over previously-persisted raw. *Observable:* re-derive over pre-existing raw rows succeeds with the original arrival source absent (file deleted, feed gone).
4. **Arrival detection without manual trigger (file-watcher surface).** A new or changed file under a registered corpus root lands as a raw artifact with provenance, unattended. *Observable:* drop/modify a file → raw artifact + provenance stamp appear within the spec's stated latency bound; identical re-arrival deduplicates (no duplicate raw identity).
5. **Binary-asset extraction (#324) through the same seam.** PDF/PPTX/XLSX/DOCX/CSV/JSON arriving in the corpus: the original binary is retained as the raw source artifact; content extraction runs as a transform under the harness conventions. *Observable:* per-format tests land binary → raw retained + extracted content present; re-extraction after extractor upgrade re-runs from the stored binary (criterion 3).
6. **Transform-harness conventions locked (ADR-16 scope).** Staging schema per source kind, transform-as-job conventions, re-derivation policy (new rows vs. update-with-audit), per-source-kind retention (long-lived vs. transient sources) are all decided in the spec/ADR. *Observable:* ADR-16's open questions 8 and 9 (architecture-overview) are dispositioned by name; a transform consumer can be written against the conventions without reading pipeline internals.
7. **Recency is a designed, measured axis (D1).** Arrival→queryable latency is instrumented per surface. *Observable:* the metric exists and is emitted in tests; the spec states the bound (the number is spec-work, not intake-work).
8. **Classification insertion point exists (D3).** The write path names the admission point where a classify-at-write stage can insert without reshaping the path. *Observable:* the Frame/spec names the point; inserting a pass-through stage there requires no schema or path change.
9. **Operations move working-state → working-state.** Watcher crash, kill mid-load, transform failure — no operation leaves state indeterminate; residue is inert; resume is safe. *Observable:* kill-mid-operation tests per pipeline stage; a re-run after any interrupt converges without manual repair. (Ingest-scoped application of the maintainer's determinacy ruling at the W2-1 gate; the general principle's canon capture is #2248, not this spec.)
10. **Fail-closed intake with designed errors.** Malformed file, unparseable binary, oversized input, decompression bomb, path-traversal name, unregistered source — each rejects or quarantines with a distinct designed error; no partial canonical write. *Observable:* an error-path test per rejection class.
11. **Missed-arrival catch-up (startup reconciliation).** Content that arrives while the watcher is down is reconciled on restart — the JTBD's "without a human noticing" holds across service outages. *Observable:* stop the watcher, add/modify a file under a registered root, restart → the file lands as a raw artifact without a re-drop. (Added at the Stage 1c fold, review finding M6; pairs with the Frame-carry reliability note.)

## Hard constraints

1. **Loader-seam share** (the maintainer, 2026-07-16, LOCKED at this intake): `1.2.0` SHALL share the `0.14.0` loader seam; the Frame decides the seam contract and which side builds first. Two drivers, two ship dates; OD-A untouched.
2. **ELT over ETL** (the maintainer, 2026-07-06, LOCKED — strangler walk): load/persist raw content FIRST (with provenance); derive as a separate RE-RUNNABLE stage; durability precedes enrichment; raw = truth, derived = disposable, regenerable projection.
3. **External-content-only ELT scope** (architecture-overview, re-scoped 2026-06-28): internal writes (host-fn storage, MCP verbs, plugin events) are not this pipeline.
4. **Classify-at-write insertability, stage not designed** (D3, ratified 2026-07-06): the write path is designed so a classification stage can insert without reshaping it; the stage itself is Tier-2-gated.
5. **Recency in scope / live-data out of identity** (D1, ratified 2026-07-06).
6. **Model-hosting posture** (P-0014 RA-5/RA-9; plus the commercial stance "call out to an LLM, never run one" — a distinct source, scoped to *generative* models): no in-process **generative** model at V0 — generative enrichment only as a policy-gated egress call-out (`EE-llm-provider` per P-0014); **local encoders are in-process and permitted** (RA-5's zero-egress config is "all four OFF + local encoders on"); the harness preserves RA-5's V0.1+ local-generative host-fn seam (no ABI change) and must not break the zero-egress configuration.
7. **Provenance via the locked envelope** (P-0015): provenance stamping uses the provenance-envelope + source-roles model — no parallel provenance scheme. Origin-weighting (PE-7) applies to ingested external content, and the new source kinds this pipeline introduces (watched-file arrival, binary-extracted content) get explicit PE-7 per-kind trust-class mapping entries at Frame — P-0015's own warning: unmapped kinds default to `background` and silently under-rank.
8. **Storage on the locked substrate** (P-0001 storage layout, P-0010 Postgres substrate engine, P-0018 core-entity manifest where entities are touched; P-0006/P-0009 — the ingest write path honors the tenant/RLS invariant; whether the standing service writes as a tenant principal or a system-scoped principal is a Frame decision).
9. **V0.1 placement stands** (OD-A, register `1.2.0`): this design does not pull ongoing ingest into V0 nor delay `0.14.0` behind it.
10. **Rust ecosystem, Green-tier dependencies only** (standing workspace constraints; binary-format extraction crates are the expected pressure point — license tier + RUSTSEC screen per the dependency-approval gate).

## Risk profile

**Touches trust boundary: YES** — external, partially-untrusted content crosses into the durable corpus, and the pipeline introduces a standing-service posture (watcher/daemon) the system doesn't have today. Required Stage-2 constraint: the security reviewer's security-mode pass runs at Frame on the known mechanism. Threat anchors carried forward: **binary-format parser attack surface** (PDF/DOCX/XLSX parsing of untrusted files; OOXML is ZIP-of-XML, so external-entity/DTD resolution — XXE with its SSRF/file-disclosure and *outbound-fetch* consequence — is in the set, and an XXE-triggered fetch would break HC6's zero-egress promise: the extraction sandbox must be **egress-denying, not merely fault-isolating** — a required Frame constraint); **resource exhaustion at intake** (oversized files, decompression bombs, watcher event floods — bounds are a required Frame constraint; the W2-1 fetch/unpack analogue); **path traversal / symlink escape** on watched roots and archive extraction; **provenance/origin trust** (adversarial corpus text must not flip trust — P-0015 PE-7 origin-weighting applies at stamp time); **standing-service authority** (the watcher runs unattended with write-through authority — its privilege scope and failure containment are Frame work); **source/root registration as admission control** (who may register a corpus root or source, and whether filesystem-write on a watched root thereby equals write-through into the durable corpus — the effective trust boundary must not silently reduce to fs-write permission on a directory); **dedup poisoning** (content-identity collisions suppressing legitimate arrivals — identity scheme is Frame work).

The D3 classify-at-write insertion point (hard constraint 4) is the designed hook for the eventual PII/data-sensitivity surface — that is *why* the insertion point exists; it fires at the first data-sensitive Tier-2 design partner (D3 gate).
